Telltale Inflation Tails: Risks From the Cross-Section of Prices in the Euro Area

In state-dependent pricing, aggregate inflation is driven by the mass of price adjustments large enough to overcome menu costs. Hence, the relevant structural object is a directional tail impulse. Yet conventional skewness confounds this signal with an unsigned kurtosis component that carries no directional information for inflation. This paper isolates this fundamental channel by estimating separately the left and right tail of relative HICP price changes from a large monthly cross-section of item prices across euro-area economies. I show that the estimated left and right tail indices predict changes in the balance of risks to inflation, while they carry little information about the conditional median. The implied indices reveal some relevant economic dynamics. An increase in the upside tail raises headline and core inflation for 18–24 months, whereas downside shocks are transitory. The indices remain significant alongside standard macro-financial controls and span information in conventional predictors.
